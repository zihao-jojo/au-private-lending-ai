# 3. Broker Ecosystem & Distribution Channels

## Broker Dominance

**76.8% of all new residential home loans** are settled through brokers (March 2025 quarter), totaling **A$99.37B**. This is far higher than the US (~25%).

Brokers are the primary distribution channel for non-bank and private lenders. Many private lenders (e.g., Aquamore) distribute **exclusively through brokers**.

## Major Aggregators

| Aggregator | Brokers | Technology Platform | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|-------|
| **Loan Market Group (LMG)** | 6,000+ | MyCRM + NextGen ApplyOnline | Formed 2023 from merger of Loan Market, PLAN, Choice, FAST |
| **AFG** | Large network | Suite360, AFG Flex (BrokerEngine Plus), SMART marketing | ASX-listed. 80+ lender panel. 29+ years |

| **SFG** | - | SFGconnect V2 | AI-generated compliance notes, client summaries |

## Aggregator Role in Private Lending

- **Panel access**: Getting on a major aggregator's panel is critical for private lenders seeking scale
- **Recent panel additions** (Connective): OwnHome, Aquamore, GCI, Asset Financier, Arch Finance — growing private lender representation
- **Broker-only distribution**: Many private lenders require borrowers to come through a broker
- **Compliance**: Aggregators provide compliance infrastructure (best interest duty, responsible lending)

## Broker Pain Points (= Opportunities)

1. **Manual document collection**: Still heavily email/paper-based
2. **Cross-lender policy comparison**: Each lender has different criteria; brokers manually match
3. **Compliance note generation**: Time-consuming but legally required
4. **Post-settlement client management**: Retention is poor
5. **Fragmented integration**: Aggregator platforms don't talk to lender systems well
6. **No AI document extraction**: For AU-specific lending documents
