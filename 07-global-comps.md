# 7. Global Comparable Companies

## Document Automation

### Ocrolus
- **What**: AI workflow platform purpose-built for lenders
- **Key stats**: 1,600+ financial document types, regulatory-grade extraction
- **Product**: Ocrolus Inspect (2024) — flags inconsistencies between application and borrower documents
- **Integration**: Encompass LOS (ICE Mortgage Technology), Blend
- **Revenue model**: Per-document or per-loan pricing
- **AU relevance**: No AU document support. Direct opportunity to build AU equivalent.

### Blend
- **What**: Digital origination platform for banks, credit unions, mortgage lenders
- **Key stats**: Powered $1.2 trillion in loan applications in 2024
- **Innovation**: "Intelligent Origination" (2025) — agentic AI in lending workflows
- **Results**: 3.5 hours saved per loan, 25% fewer underwriting turns, 50% less borrower withdrawal
- **AU relevance**: US-focused. Australian aggregator CRMs are the local equivalent but far less sophisticated.

### Snapdocs
- **What**: #1 eClosing platform in US
- **Key stats**: Powers 1-in-4 US mortgage transactions
- **Products**: Digital closing (wet, hybrid, eNote, RON), eVault, Notary Connect
- **AU relevance**: PEXA handles electronic settlement in AU but no equivalent eClosing experience.

### Inscribe
- **What**: Document fraud detection (created the category in 2017)
- **Key stats**: 1 in 16 documents shows fraud signs; AI-generated fraud up 5x in 2025
- **Results**: Bluevine prevented $150K fraud on single application; Plaid reduced review from 1-2 days to <30 seconds
- **AU relevance**: No AU equivalent. Critical gap as AI-generated document fraud grows globally.

## Underwriting Automation

### Zest AI
- **What**: ML-powered credit decisioning
- **Key stats**: ~300 lenders, 25% more approvals, 20% fewer defaults, automates 60-80% of decisions
- **Products**: LuLu (GenAI lending intelligence), Zest Protect (fraud)
- **Insight**: 83% of lenders plan to increase GenAI budgets in 2026
- **AU relevance**: No AU equivalent. Biggest gap in the market.

### Upstart
- **What**: AI lending platform partnering with banks
- **Key stats**: Evaluates 1,000+ data points beyond traditional credit scores
- **Focus**: Personal loans, auto loans, credit cards. Expanding credit access to thin-file borrowers.
- **AU relevance**: AU has similar thin-file problem (new migrants, self-employed, gig workers).

### LoanPro
- **What**: Composable, API-first loan management system
- **Products**: Scalable automation, customizable workflows, servicing
- **AU relevance**: finPOWER Connect is the closest AU equivalent but not AI-powered.

### Better (Tinman Engine)
- **What**: Proprietary loan origination engine
- **Innovation**: Completes initial underwriting autonomously, eliminates appraisal delays
- **AU relevance**: Tiimely and Nano are closest AU equivalents.

### HomeVision (MIRA)
- **What**: Machine intelligence for collateral, income, assets, and credit decisioning
- **Results**: Doubled operational efficiency in collateral underwriting
- **Timeline**: Rolling out 2026

## Property Valuation (AVM)

### HouseCanary (US)
- **Coverage**: 136M+ US properties
- **Innovation**: Computer vision for room type identification and condition assessment
- **Key insight**: Only ~40% of homes can be evaluated solely by AVM
- **AU equivalent**: CoreLogic and PropTrack dominate but opportunity in non-standard properties

### Quantarium (US)
- **What**: Self-learning AI engine for residential valuation
- **Innovation**: Continuously improves accuracy as data ingested
- **AU equivalent**: No self-learning AVM in AU market

## Key Takeaway

The US lending tech ecosystem is 5-7 years ahead of Australia. The AU market has:
- **No Ocrolus** (document extraction)
- **No Zest AI** (AI underwriting)
- **No Inscribe** (fraud detection)
- **No Blend-level agentic AI** (workflow automation)

Each of these represents a standalone business opportunity in the AU market, especially as:
1. CDR/Open Banking creates new data pipes
2. CCR provides richer credit data
3. Private credit grows at 9%+ annually
4. Banks continue retreating from segments
